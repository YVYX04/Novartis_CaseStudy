

# Novartis Case Study
## Prototyping an AI Marketing Copilot for Omnichannel Transformation

University of St. Gallen, Spring 2026

## Overview

This repository contains the working materials for our Novartis case study project in the context of the University of St. Gallen course on strategy communication and implementation. The objective of the project is not to build a production-ready AI product, but to design a realistic and convincing demo of an internal AI-powered copilot that could support Novartis marketeers as they transition toward a more data-driven, personalized, and omnichannel way of working.

The repository is therefore structured as a presentation and prototyping workspace. It will be used to develop the strategic concept, the demo interface, the supporting narrative, and the presentation materials required to pitch our recommendation.

## Project Objective

Our case focuses on the following strategic question:

How can Novartis support junior and senior marketeers in adopting new AI- and data-supported marketing practices, while accelerating omnichannel client engagement across the Western European Cluster?

To address this question, we are building a fake but credible demo of an internal AI agent. The goal is to make the solution feel operationally realistic, strategically relevant, and adapted to the constraints of a large pharmaceutical company.

## What This Repository Will Contain

This repository will progressively gather all the assets required for the project, including:

- the conceptual framing of the solution;
- the messaging and change-management rationale behind the proposal;
- the local front-end prototype built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript;
- presentation slides and visual assets for the final pitch;
- draft outputs used in the demo, such as campaign recommendations, coaching toolkits, field activation support, and KPI dashboards.

## Core Idea of the Demo

The central artifact of the project is a local demo of an AI marketing copilot. Rather than functioning as a real AI system, the prototype will simulate realistic user interactions through pre-scripted workflows and outputs.

The prototype is intended to demonstrate how such a tool could help Novartis teams:

- design omnichannel campaign plans;
- adapt messaging across channels and personas;
- support senior marketeers in coaching their teams;
- align marketing and field execution;
- track adoption and transformation KPIs.

In other words, the demo is not only a marketing tool demonstration. It is also a change-management device designed to show how AI can be embedded into new ways of working.

## Working Principles

A few principles guide the work in this repository.

First, the prototype must remain simple enough to be executed reliably during a live presentation. Second, the output must feel realistic in a Novartis environment, which means that the interface, language, and workflows should reflect the logic of pharmaceutical marketing and internal transformation. Third, the project must remain presentation-oriented: credibility, clarity, and strategic coherence matter more than technical sophistication.

## Deliverable

The final deliverable of the project will be a consulting-style pitch showing:

1. the communication campaign concept;
2. the role of generative AI in the transformation;
3. the structure and rationale of the proposed program;
4. a realistic demo of the AI-enabled solution;
5. the KPIs that would be used to evaluate adoption and impact.
